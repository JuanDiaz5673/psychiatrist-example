About Dr. Mitchell

Care that treats the whole person — not just the diagnosis.

Dr. Sarah Mitchell is a board-certified psychiatrist with over a decade of experience helping patients navigate mood disorders, anxiety, ADHD, and life transitions. Her practice is built around collaborative, evidence-based treatment plans that respect each patient's goals, values, and life context.

She believes that good psychiatric care is a conversation, not a prescription pad — and that every patient deserves a clinician who actually listens.

What I treat

Services & areas of focus

A focused practice serving adults and adolescents (14+). Visits combine careful medication management with brief, supportive psychotherapy.

Depression

Major depressive disorder, persistent depressive disorder, postpartum, and treatment-resistant depression.

Anxiety disorders

Generalized anxiety, panic, social anxiety, and specific phobias. Both pharmacologic and behavioral approaches.

ADHD

Comprehensive evaluation and ongoing management for adolescents and adults newly seeking diagnosis or transferring care.

Bipolar disorder

Long-term mood stabilization with careful monitoring and patient-centered planning around life events.

PTSD & trauma

Trauma-informed evaluation and pharmacologic support, coordinated with your therapist if you have one.

Medication management

Initial assessment, titration, side-effect monitoring, and ongoing follow-up — typically every 1–3 months.

Telehealth visits

Secure video appointments available statewide in Oregon and Washington for established patients.

Adolescent psychiatry

Care for patients ages 14–18, with family involvement when appropriate. Coordinated with schools and pediatricians.

Second opinions

If you're unsure about a current treatment plan, I offer one-time consultations to review and advise.

My approach

"My job isn't to hand you a label — it's to understand what's actually going on in your life and figure out, together, the smallest change that helps the most."

— Dr. Sarah Mitchell, MD
